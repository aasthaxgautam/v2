The Problem

When I joined as Co-founder, our marketing operations were typical of an early-stage B2B company:

What wasn't working:

  • Data Silos: Marketing campaigns tracked in spreadsheets, sales data in HubSpot CRM, no connection between the two
  • Manual Reporting: Spent 10+ hours per week manually pulling data from multiple sources to create weekly reports
  • No Lead Scoring: Sales team treated all leads equally, wasting time on low-intent prospects
  • Unclear Attribution: Couldn't tell which channels or campaigns were driving actual conversions
  • Reactive Approach: Only discovered campaign underperformance weeks after launch
The Business Impact: Marketing spend was increasing 15% quarter-over-quarter, but we couldn't prove ROI. The sales team was frustrated with lead quality, and I was spending more time reporting than optimizing.

My Solution

I took a systematic approach to rebuilding our marketing operations infrastructure in HubSpot:

Phase 1: Audit & Diagnosis (Week 1-2)

  • Mapped existing tech stack and data flows
  • Interviewed sales team to understand lead quality issues
  • Analyzed 6 months of campaign data to identify patterns
  • Documented current workflows and pain points

Phase 2: HubSpot Optimization (Week 3-8)

1. Automated Lead Scoring System

  • Built behavioral scoring model based on: website activity, content downloads, email engagement, form submissions
  • Created demographic scoring for company size, industry, job title
  • Established clear thresholds: Cold (0-30), Warm (31-60), Hot (61-100)
  • Automated assignment of MQL status at score threshold

2. Workflow Automation

  • Designed 12 automated workflows including: lead nurture sequences, sales notifications for hot leads, re-engagement campaigns for cold contacts
  • Built automated segmentation based on lifecycle stage and engagement level
  • Created trigger-based email sequences for specific user behaviors

3. Unified Dashboards

  • Built executive dashboard showing: MQLs by source, conversion rates by channel, pipeline velocity, campaign ROI
  • Created sales dashboard with: hot leads requiring follow-up, engagement scores, account history
  • Implemented weekly automated reports sent to stakeholders

Phase 3: Testing & Optimization (Week 9-16)

  • A/B tested email sequences and landing pages
  • Refined lead scoring model based on actual conversion data
  • Optimized workflow triggers to reduce noise for sales team
  • Built feedback loop with sales to continuously improve lead quality

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Sales Team Resistance

Problem: Sales reps were skeptical of automated lead scoring, preferring their "gut feel"

Solution: Ran a 30-day pilot where I tracked performance of scored vs. unscored leads. Data showed scored leads converted 35% faster. Presented results in weekly sales meeting, won buy-in.

Challenge 2: Data Quality Issues

Problem: Historical contact data was messy with duplicates, missing fields, inconsistent formatting

Solution: Built data cleaning workflows with validation rules. Spent two weeks manually cleaning critical fields. Implemented going-forward standards for data entry.

Challenge 3: Limited HubSpot Knowledge

Problem: Team wasn't familiar with HubSpot's advanced features

Solution: Created internal documentation and SOPs. Ran weekly training sessions. Built a #hubspot-help Slack channel for ongoing support.

Key Learnings

1. Start with the Problem, Not the Tool

I didn't jump straight into building workflows. I spent two weeks understanding what was actually broken and what success looked like. This prevented building automation for automation's sake.

2. Incremental Wins Build Momentum

Rather than launching everything at once, I rolled out features progressively. Quick wins (like the first automated dashboard) built trust and enthusiasm for the larger transformation.

3. Sales + Marketing Alignment is Everything

The lead scoring system only worked because I co-created it with the sales team. Their input on what makes a "good lead" was critical to building something they'd actually use.

4. Documentation Scales Your Impact

Building SOPs and training materials meant the system could run without me constantly troubleshooting. It also made it easier to onboard new team members.
