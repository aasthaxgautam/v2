Project Overview

The Challenge Bloomlyne needed to build a predictable B2B pipeline to support ambitious growth targets. We had a great product and talented sales team, but lacked the operational infrastructure to generate and qualify leads at scale. Marketing and sales were working in separate silos, and we had no standardized process for lead handoff or follow-up.

The Problem

When I started analyzing our go-to-market operations, I discovered several critical bottlenecks:

Marketing Challenges:

  • Limited inbound volume: Only 20-30 leads per month, mostly unqualified
  • No multi-channel strategy: Relying solely on organic LinkedIn posts and word-of-mouth
  • Inconsistent messaging: No standardized value propositions or positioning
  • Poor content strategy: Sporadic content creation with no clear conversion path

Sales Challenges:

  • No sales enablement materials: Reps creating their own pitch decks and one-pagers
  • Inefficient prospecting: Spending hours researching prospects with no templates or playbooks
  • Unclear qualification criteria: Sales pursuing leads that were unlikely to close
  • Long sales cycles: Average 90+ days from first touch to close

Operations Challenges:

  • Manual handoffs: Marketing emailing leads to sales via spreadsheets
  • No lead nurturing: Cold leads disappeared into a black hole
  • Zero visibility: Couldn't track which marketing activities drove sales outcomes

My Solution: Building a Multi-Channel Lead Generation Engine

Phase 1: Foundation - Strategy & Infrastructure (Months 1-2)

Step 1: Defined Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Worked with sales and leadership to identify our best-fit customers:

  • Industry: Manufacturing, logistics, supply chain companies
  • Company size: 50-500 employees, €5M-€50M revenue
  • Pain points: Manual processes, legacy systems, scaling challenges
  • Decision makers: Operations Directors, IT Managers, CEOs

Step 2: Built Lead Qualification Framework

Created BANT-based qualification criteria that both marketing and sales agreed on:

  • Budget: €20K+ annual tech budget
  • Authority: Decision maker or strong influencer
  • Need: Active pain point in operations/tech stack
  • Timeline: Looking to implement within 6 months

Step 3: Created Sales Enablement Assets

Built standardized materials so sales could focus on selling:

  • Master pitch deck (customizable for industries)
  • One-page service overviews for each offering
  • Customer case studies and testimonials
  • ROI calculator templates
  • Email templates for common scenarios
  • Objection handling guide

Phase 3: Operations & Optimization (Months 7-10)

Built End-to-End Lead Management System

  • Automated lead routing: Hot leads instantly assigned to sales reps based on territory
  • Lead nurturing workflows: Automated email sequences for each stage (awareness, consideration, decision)
  • Sales notifications: Real-time alerts when leads hit qualification thresholds
  • Pipeline tracking: Full visibility from first touch to closed deal

Established Weekly Marketing-Sales Sync

  • Weekly meetings to review lead quality and conversion rates
  • Captured sales feedback on lead fit and readiness
  • Adjusted targeting and messaging based on what resonated
  • Celebrated wins and learned from losses

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Limited Marketing Budget

Problem: We had no budget for paid ads or expensive marketing automation tools

Solution: Focused on organic channels (LinkedIn, email) and leveraged free/low-cost tools. Built content in-house. Used creativity over cash to stand out.

Challenge 2: Sales Adoption of New Materials

Problem: Some sales reps preferred their old methods and resisted using new templates

Solution: Involved top performers in creating the materials so they had buy-in. Showcased early wins in team meetings. Made materials easily customizable.

Challenge 3: Long Sales Cycles

Problem: B2B deals in our industry took 90+ days even with better leads

Solution: Built middle-of-funnel nurture content to keep prospects engaged. Created "quick win" offerings to get smaller commitments faster. Shortened average cycle to 60-70 days.

Detailed Results

Pipeline Metrics:

  • 30% increase in qualified pipeline – From ~€200K to €260K average quarterly pipeline
  • 3x increase in sales-ready leads – From 10 to 30+ qualified leads per month
  • 25% faster lead-to-MQL conversion – From 21 days to 16 days average
  • 40% improvement in close rate – Better qualified leads meant higher win rates

Channel Performance:

  • LinkedIn: 60% of inbound leads attributed to thought leadership content
  • Email outbound: 25% of pipeline from targeted sequences
  • Content marketing: 15% of pipeline from gated resources

Efficiency Gains:

  • Sales enablement: Reps saved 5+ hours/week not creating materials from scratch
  • Lead handoff: Automated routing eliminated manual email coordination
  • Reporting: Real-time dashboards replaced weekly manual reports

Key Learnings

1. Operations Enables Execution

The best marketing strategy fails without operational infrastructure. Lead routing, nurturing, and tracking aren't "nice to haves" - they're the foundation that makes campaigns actually convert.

2. Sales-Marketing Alignment is Non-Negotiable

I spent 30% of my time on alignment meetings, feedback sessions, and building trust between teams. This investment paid off 10x when both teams pulled in the same direction.

3. Test Small, Scale What Works

Rather than launching all channels at once, we started with LinkedIn, proved it worked, then added email, then content. This allowed us to optimize each channel before adding complexity.
